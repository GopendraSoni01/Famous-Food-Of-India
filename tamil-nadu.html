Famous Foods of Tamil Nadu

Idli

Idli Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
    • 2 cups rice
    • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • Water as needed
  • Instructions:
    1. Soak the urad dal and fenugreek seeds in water for 4-6 hours.
    2. Soak the rice in water for 4-6 hours.
    3. Drain and grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds into a smooth batter.
    4. Grind the rice into a slightly coarse batter.
    5. Mix both batters together, add salt, and let it ferment overnight.
    6. Steam the batter in idli molds for about 10-15 minutes.
    7. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Sambar

Sambar Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 1 tomato, chopped
    • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, potatoes)
    • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 tablespoon oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Chopped coriander for garnish
  • Instructions:
    1. Cook toor dal in a pressure cooker until soft.
    2. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter.
    3. Add chopped onions and tomatoes, sauté until soft.
    4. Add mixed vegetables, turmeric powder, sambar powder, and salt. Cook until vegetables are tender.
    5. Add cooked dal and adjust consistency with water if needed. Simmer for a few minutes.
    6. Garnish with chopped coriander and serve with idli or dosa.

Chettinad Chicken

Chettinad Chicken Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
    • 2 onions, finely chopped
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
    • 2 tablespoons Chettinad masala (or garam masala)
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 3 tablespoons oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander for garnish
  • Instructions:
    1. Heat oil in a pan, add chopped onions, and sauté until golden brown.
    2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
    3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
    4. Add chicken pieces, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the chicken is browned.
    5. Add Chettinad masala and a little water, cover, and simmer until the chicken is cooked through.
    6. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with rice or naan.
