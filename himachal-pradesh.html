Famous Foods of Himachal Pradesh

Chana Madra

Chana Madra Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 cup chana (chickpeas), soaked overnight
    • 2 tablespoons ghee
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 cup yogurt, whisked
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander for garnish
  • Instructions:
    1. Heat ghee in a pan and add cumin seeds.
    2. Add onions and cook until golden brown.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
    4. Add soaked chana and cook for a few minutes.
    5. Add yogurt, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
    6. Simmer until chana is cooked and the gravy thickens.
    7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with rice or roti.

Dham

Dham Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 cup rice
    • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
    • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
    • 1/4 cup curd
    • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 green chili, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon ghee
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander for garnish
  • Instructions:
    1. Soak rice, urad dal, and chana dal in water for 2 hours.
    2. Drain and grind into a coarse paste.
    3. Heat ghee in a pan and add mustard seeds and cumin seeds.
    4. Add chopped green chili and sauté for a minute.
    5. Add the dal mixture, turmeric powder, and salt.
    6. Cook until the mixture thickens.
    7. Mix in curd and cook for a few more minutes.
    8. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.

Siddu

Siddu Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 2 cups whole wheat flour
    • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
    • 1/2 cup raisins
    • 1/4 cup jaggery, grated
    • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
    • Water as needed
  • Instructions:
    1. Mix whole wheat flour, fennel seeds, cardamom powder, and baking soda in a bowl.
    2. Add walnuts, raisins, and jaggery. Mix well.
    3. Add water gradually to form a soft dough.
    4. Divide the dough into small balls and flatten them slightly.
    5. Steam the flattened dough in a steamer for about 15-20 minutes.
    6. Serve hot with ghee or butter.
