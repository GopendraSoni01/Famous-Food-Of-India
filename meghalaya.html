Famous Foods of Meghalaya

Jadoh

Jadoh Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 2 cups rice
    • 500 grams pork, cut into pieces
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon black pepper powder
    • 2 tablespoons oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Coriander leaves for garnish
  • Instructions:
    1. Heat oil in a large pot and sauté onions until golden brown.
    2. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for a minute.
    3. Add pork pieces, turmeric powder, black pepper powder, and salt. Cook until the pork is browned.
    4. Add rice and mix well. Pour in enough water to cover the rice and pork.
    5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until rice and pork are cooked through and water is absorbed.
    6. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.

Phuklein

Phuklein Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 cup rice flour
    • 1 cup grated coconut
    • 1/2 cup jaggery, grated
    • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1/2 cup water (or as needed)
    • Oil for frying
  • Instructions:
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine rice flour, grated coconut, jaggery, cardamom powder, and baking soda.
    2. Gradually add water to form a smooth, thick batter.
    3. Heat oil in a pan for frying. Drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil.
    4. Fry until golden brown and crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
    5. Let cool slightly before serving.

Tungrymbai

Tungrymbai Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 200 grams fermented soybeans
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 tablespoon oil
    • Salt to taste
    • Coriander leaves for garnish
  • Instructions:
    1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté onions until translucent.
    2. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Cook for a minute.
    3. Add fermented soybeans and mix well. Cook for 5-7 minutes.
    4. Season with salt and garnish with coriander leaves.
    5. Serve warm with rice or as a side dish.
