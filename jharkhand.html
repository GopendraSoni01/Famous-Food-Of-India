Famous Foods of Jharkhand

Rugda

Rugda Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 cup Rugda (dried mushrooms)
    • 1 tablespoon mustard oil
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 tomato, chopped
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander for garnish
  • Instructions:
    1. Soak Rugda in warm water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
    2. Heat mustard oil in a pan and add chopped onions. Cook until golden brown.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
    4. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, cumin powder, and coriander powder. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
    5. Add the soaked Rugda and cook for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    6. Season with salt and garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot with rice or chapati.

Thekua

Thekua Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 cup wheat flour
    • 1/2 cup jaggery, grated
    • 1/4 cup coconut, grated
    • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
    • Water as needed
  • Instructions:
    1. Mix wheat flour, jaggery, grated coconut, cardamom powder, and baking soda in a bowl.
    2. Add ghee or oil and mix well.
    3. Add water gradually and knead into a stiff dough.
    4. Shape the dough into small discs or oval shapes.
    5. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the Thekua until golden brown and crisp.
    6. Drain on paper towels and let cool before serving.

Chaapar

Chaapar Recipe:

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 cup rice flour
    • 1/2 cup grated coconut
    • 1/2 cup jaggery, grated
    • 1/4 cup water
    • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
    • A pinch of salt
  • Instructions:
    1. Mix rice flour, grated coconut, jaggery, cardamom powder, and salt in a bowl.
    2. Add water gradually and mix until it forms a dough-like consistency.
    3. Shape the mixture into small round balls or discs.
    4. Steam the balls or discs in a steamer for about 15-20 minutes.
    5. Allow to cool slightly before serving.
